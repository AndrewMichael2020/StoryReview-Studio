Scene 1: Cold Open

Maria stands in the fluorescent hush of the packing line, the clock above the exit ticking toward 11:58 p.m.

The machines have been silent for an hour now. Everyone knows what tomorrow brings, but tonight they pretend not to.

"Three hundred people would be out of work by morning," the memo said. Maria had read it twice, the paper trembling slightly in her hands.

She had worked at River Mill for seventeen years. First as a line worker, then as a shift supervisor. The plant was where she'd met her husband, raised her kids on the health insurance, planned her retirement.

The town never forgave the company for what happened next.
